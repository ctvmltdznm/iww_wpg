With the appointment of Woldemar Hommel in 1920 as professor of Metallography and Chemical Technology, the systematic study of the structures and properties of metallic materials began at the then Mining Academy. Present and Perspectives


The Institute for Materials Science and Engineering sets a new focus on digitalization in materials science and engineering.


Today, the Institute for Materials Science and Engineering is comprehensively dedicated to the behaviour of materials and substances. Since the appointment of Prof. Uwe Wolfram in October 2023, a new emphasis has been placed on digitalization in materials science and engineering.

This includes the detailed investigation of material behaviour in biological tissues and organs, with particular attention to mineral-reinforced biopolymers, bioceramics, and bioglasses as well as their composites. The material architectures of these systems are central to our interest. We focus on these materials because they are intrinsically circular, produce no non-reusable waste, and exhibit properties that are equivalent or even superior to those of technical materials.

To study the material properties, we use a combination of innovative experiments, numerical methods, and mathematical models â€” ranging from the molecular to the macroscopic length scale.

The digital representation of material architectures and the associated algorithms for data processing and interpretation are essential tools to understand the relationship between process, function, and material architecture. By processes, we refer to both technical processes and pathophysiological processes from biomedical contexts.

Our experiments include not only established laboratory analytical methods such as XRD, EDX, ÂµCT, SEM, and XRF, but also in-depth investigations at large-scale research facilities such as synchrotrons.
